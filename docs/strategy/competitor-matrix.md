# Recall Signals competitor matrix

Research checked on **June 20, 2026**. Prices are public list prices from vendor websites, exclude tax, and may vary by billing term or negotiated contract.

## Positioning summary

Recall Signals sits between three established categories:

1. **Creative workflow tools** collect competitor ads and help teams produce briefs.
2. **Social listening and market intelligence tools** monitor what is being published and discussed at scale.
3. **Personal knowledge tools** preserve items an individual deliberately saves.

The commercial opening is agency knowledge that currently falls between those categories: the high-intent posts that strategists, creators, and account leads personally judge worth saving, but which never become a reusable team asset.

> **Core distinction:** Social listening finds what is popular. Recall preserves what your team believes matters.

## Recall Signals wedge

| Dimension | Recall Signals position |
|---|---|
| Input | Posts and references intentionally saved by agency staff |
| Signal | Human judgment first; AI enrichment second |
| Analysis | Semantic search, entities, topics, and connections across selected material |
| Output | Source-linked intelligence boards now; automated cited briefs in the agency pilot |
| Buyer | Creative strategy, social, content, and account teams inside agencies |
| Commercial value | Faster research, less duplicated searching, stronger evidence in client recommendations |
| Defensibility | A growing private corpus of what the agency's best people considered meaningful |

## Messaging guardrails

- Do not describe Recall as a social listening replacement. It is a complementary high-intent signal layer.
- Do not imply that team workspaces, roles, shared editing, or automated brief generation are live before they ship.
- Do not compete on the number of indexed public ads or mentions.
- Do compete on provenance, collective taste, cross-client learning, and the path from saved reference to defensible recommendation.
- Avoid consumer "second brain" language in agency acquisition campaigns. Lead with creative intelligence, research reuse, and client-ready evidence.

## Pricing implication

The proposed **Studio £149/mo**, **Agency £349/mo**, and **Agency Plus £749/mo** tiers sit:

- above personal knowledge tools because the buyer is funding a team workflow and client output;
- below enterprise market-intelligence platforms such as Feedly;
- near creative workflow software, while differentiating on human-curated organic and cross-platform signals.

The price should be validated against time saved and client value during the pilot, not defended through feature count alone.
