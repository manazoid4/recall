# Recall Signals: 90-day revenue roadmap

Start date: **June 22, 2026**

End date: **September 19, 2026**

## 90-day outcome

Prove that agencies will pay for a workflow that turns team-saved social posts into client-ready creative intelligence.

### Commercial goals

- Recruit **8 design partners**.
- Activate **6 agency pilots**.
- Convert **4 pilots to paid plans**.
- Reach **£1,196-£2,096 MRR**, depending on the Studio/Agency mix.
- Process at least **2,000 source-linked saves** across pilot accounts.
- Produce **30 client-facing intelligence boards or briefs**.
- Capture **3 publishable case studies** with quantified time or quality outcomes.

### Product proof goals

- A new pilot team reaches its first useful intelligence board within **48 hours**.
- At least **60% of invited pilot users** save, enrich, search, or curate an item weekly.
- At least **70% of created boards** retain source links for every recommendation.
- Pilot users rate the output at least **8/10 for usefulness** in a real client workflow.

## Founding agency pilot offer

### Who it is for

Independent creative, social, content, and performance agencies with:

- 5-40 staff;
- at least 3 people regularly saving social inspiration;
- recurring client strategy or creative briefing work;
- one live client project suitable for a four-week pilot.

### Offer

- Four-week guided pilot.
- Up to 5 active users during the pilot.
- One live client intelligence workflow.
- Import and organization support for the team's initial saved-post corpus.
- Weekly 30-minute working session.
- Direct access to the product roadmap.
- Pilot fee: **£500**, credited in full against the first three paid months if the agency converts.
- Conversion options: **Studio £149/mo**, **Agency £349/mo**, or **Agency Plus £749/mo**.

### Success agreement

Before kickoff, agree on:

- the client or pitch being supported;
- the current research and briefing process;
- baseline hours spent gathering and organizing references;
- the output to produce;
- the date the output will be used;
- the decision-maker who will review conversion.

Do not start unpaid, open-ended pilots. If a strategic design partner cannot pay, exchange the fee only for a signed case-study commitment and scheduled weekly access.

### Weekly leading indicators

- 20 new qualified agency contacts added.
- 15 personalized first-touch messages sent.
- 10 personalized follow-ups sent.
- 2 discovery calls booked.
- 1 pilot proposal sent.
- 1 customer workflow observed or reviewed.
- 1 new proof asset published or added to the sales library.

### Activation metrics

- Time from account creation to first imported item.
- Time to first enriched item.
- Time to first search result used in a client workflow.
- Time to first board with at least 10 cited items.
- Number of active contributors per workspace.
- Saves, searches, and board additions per active user.

### Revenue metrics

- Pilot fees collected.
- New MRR and expansion MRR.
- Pilot-to-paid conversion rate.
- Average revenue per agency.
- Sales-cycle length.
- Gross revenue retention.
- Founder hours spent per activated agency.

## Weekly execution plan

### Week 1 — June 22-28: package the sale

- Publish agency-first landing and pricing pages.
- Create a one-page pilot brief using the offer above.
- Build a target-account template with agency size, services, clients, decision-maker, and visible research behavior.
- Select the first 40 UK agencies from creative, social, content, and performance niches.
- Record a five-minute demo showing capture → patterns → cited board using a realistic client topic.
- Goal: 40 targets, 15 messages, 2 calls booked.

### Week 2 — June 29-July 5: run problem interviews

- Conduct 4-5 discovery calls.
- Ask each agency to screen-share its current save, swipe-file, research, and briefing process.
- Quantify duplicated research time, lost references, and approval friction.
- Test the distinction between social listening and team-selected signal.
- Revise the demo around the strongest repeated pain.
- Goal: 5 calls completed, 3 qualified opportunities, 1 pilot proposal.

### Week 3 — July 6-12: close the first design partners

- Send proposals within 24 hours of each qualified call.
- Require one named client workflow and a kickoff date.
- Offer the £500 credited pilot without discounting the future subscription.
- Prepare manual onboarding for exports, URLs, and Instagram provenance.
- Goal: 2 signed pilots, £1,000 pilot revenue.

### Week 4 — July 13-19: deliver first value

- Import each team's initial corpus.
- Create a board taxonomy around client, audience tension, hook, format, and evidence strength.
- Build the first intelligence board live with the team.
- Measure time to first useful board and compare it with the prior process.
- Goal: both pilots activated within 48 hours and one client output used.

### Week 5 — July 20-26: tighten activation

- Review where users fail to capture, enrich, search, or curate.
- Create a 15-minute onboarding checklist.
- Add concierge support where product gaps remain.
- Begin recruiting the next two pilots using evidence from the first cohort.
- Goal: 60% weekly active invited users and 2 additional proposals.

### Week 6 — July 27-August 2: exploit category movement

- Publish a timely comparison for teams affected by MagicBrief's **July 31, 2026** shutdown, without claiming full migration or feature parity.
- Position Recall around organic references, source provenance, and agency knowledge retention.
- Contact agencies publicly discussing creative research tools.
- Goal: 20 targeted conversations, 3 calls, 1 new pilot.

### Week 7 — August 3-9: prove the briefing outcome

- Observe a real strategy or briefing meeting.
- Record which sources are opened, challenged, or reused.
- Prototype the minimum cited brief export using the existing board workflow plus manual service.
- Measure revision rounds and stakeholder confidence.
- Goal: 5 client-ready boards/briefs produced and usefulness score of 8/10.

### Week 8 — August 10-16: convert cohort one

- Hold commercial reviews before each four-week pilot ends.
- Present usage, time saved, outputs created, and agreed next workflow.
- Convert the right account to Studio or Agency based on team size and client separation needs.
- Ask converted customers for one referral and case-study permission.
- Goal: 2 paid conversions and first MRR.

### Week 9 — August 17-23: build repeatable proof

- Publish the first quantified case study.
- Turn objections into a short sales FAQ.
- Create three outbound variants for creative agencies, social agencies, and performance agencies.
- Remove messages that attract individual consumers rather than team buyers.
- Goal: 45 outbound touches, 4 calls, 2 proposals.

### Week 10 — August 24-30: validate pricing

- Ask every qualified buyer what budget owns the problem and what alternative spend Recall displaces.
- Test Studio against small specialist teams and Agency against multi-client teams.
- Do not reduce list prices; adjust scope, onboarding, or pilot credit first.
- Track whether Agency Plus demand is real or premature.
- Goal: 2 new pilots and a documented pricing decision.

### Week 11 — August 31-September 6: reduce founder service load

- Convert repeated onboarding work into templates and in-product guidance.
- Define a standard first-board recipe.
- Set a maximum founder-support budget per pilot.
- Identify the collaboration feature most responsible for conversion and prioritize only that feature.
- Goal: activation support below 3 founder hours per new agency.

### Week 12 — September 7-13: close the second cohort

- Run commercial reviews for all remaining pilots.
- Use annual prepay only as a closing option after monthly value is accepted.
- Request referrals from successful users to peer agencies.
- Goal: 2 additional paid conversions and 4 total paid agencies.

### Week 13 — September 14-19: decide the next quarter

- Review funnel conversion, usage, support load, and retention risk.
- Segment wins by agency type and use case.
- Decide whether to focus the next 90 days on one niche, broaden acquisition, or pause sales to close a critical collaboration gap.
- Publish an internal revenue memo with actuals against every target in this plan.
- Goal: £1,196-£2,096 MRR, 3 case studies, and one clear ideal customer profile.

## Discovery questions

1. Show me where your team saves creative references today.
2. What happens when a strategist needs those references for a client brief?
3. How often does the team repeat research someone has already done?
4. Which part of a recommendation is hardest to defend to a client?
5. What do your listening or ad-library tools miss?
6. What would make a shared intelligence board worth paying for each month?
7. Who owns the budget, and what existing spend would this replace or improve?

## Conversion criteria

Convert a pilot only when the agency has:

- used Recall in a real client workflow;
- created at least two useful source-linked outputs;
- identified an internal owner;
- agreed on the next monthly use case;
- accepted the relevant list price.

Treat low usage as a product or qualification signal, not a reason to extend the pilot indefinitely.
