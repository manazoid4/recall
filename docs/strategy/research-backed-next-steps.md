# Recall Signals: research-backed next steps

Research snapshot: **June 21, 2026**

## Decision

Build the shortest credible path from captured evidence to paid agency learning.

The next product should not be another set of disconnected pages. Each release should strengthen this chain:

> capture → explain → group → recommend → assign → deliver → measure → reuse

## What the 100-product landscape changes

### 1. Execution Router is valuable only when grounded in evidence

Zapier, Make, n8n, Gumloop, Relay, Lindy, Relevance AI and Stack AI can all coordinate generic work. Recall cannot win as a generic agent router.

It can win when every routed task carries:

- the client;
- the supporting sources;
- the human annotation;
- the confidence/evidence-strength basis;
- the expected deliverable;
- the result after delivery.

### 2. The first durable moat is client-scoped learning

Competitors can copy AI summaries and prompt generation. They cannot immediately copy an agency's accumulated relationship between:

- who saved a source;
- why they saved it;
- which client it affected;
- what pattern it supported;
- what recommendation followed;
- what creative was delivered;
- what happened in market.

This requires tenant and client isolation before broad feature expansion.

### 3. Recall should integrate with category leaders, not recreate them

- Link to official ad libraries instead of indexing the whole market.
- Import selected listening evidence instead of building a firehose.
- Export briefs to Notion and Markdown instead of replacing general documentation.
- Accept outcome metrics from reporting tools instead of building a BI platform.
- Hand off approved work to automation systems instead of becoming an automation engine.

### 4. Human judgment must remain a first-class object

Recall's strongest distinction disappears if every field becomes an AI-generated label. The product must visibly preserve:

- who noticed it;
- why it mattered;
- whether the statement is source fact, human annotation or AI inference;
- what evidence contradicts it;
- who approved the recommendation.

### 5. Revenue depends on repeated client workflows

The free Router is acquisition. Monthly value begins when agencies need:

- shared history;
- reusable templates;
- permissions;
- client separation;
- recurring reports;
- approvals;
- outcome tracking;
- retained learning after staff turnover.

## Ranked build sequence

### Priority 0 — Production trust baseline

Do before inviting paid pilots:

- correct pooled database configuration;
- remove known critical/high dependency findings through a tested upgrade;
- verify Clerk production mode;
- add production-safe health checks;
- complete authenticated desktop/mobile QA;
- confirm deletion, retention and audit foundations;
- verify no cross-tenant or cross-client access.

Commercial reason: agencies will not pay monthly for a workflow that can lose, leak or fail to retrieve research.

### Priority 1 — Agency workspace and client isolation

Build:

- workspace;
- member;
- role;
- client/project;
- contributor attribution;
- permission-aware search;
- client-scoped boards;
- persisted Router plans;
- activity history.

Commercial reason: this converts a personal tool into a £149-£349/month team system.

### Priority 2 — Evidence-to-brief engine

Build:

- structured annotations;
- claim type: source fact / human annotation / AI inference;
- pattern clusters;
- independent-source count;
- contradiction capture;
- evidence strength;
- cited recommendation blocks;
- Markdown/PDF/Notion-compatible brief export.

Commercial reason: this produces the client-facing artifact that justifies spend.

### Priority 3 — Capture activation loop

Build:

- reliable explicit extension capture;
- URL paste;
- mobile share workflow;
- JSON/export imports;
- screenshots/files;
- “why I saved this”;
- client assignment at capture;
- contributor identity;
- per-item retry and partial success.

Commercial reason: a workspace without a low-friction corpus never reaches value.

### Priority 4 — Outcome and validated-learning loop

Build:

- recommendation status;
- delivered-creative link;
- campaign/result fields;
- outcome source and date;
- successful/failed/inconclusive result;
- reusable learning;
- “used before” context;
- evidence-strength adjustment from outcomes.

Commercial reason: this creates retention and the strongest long-term data moat.

### Priority 5 — Pilot and billing operations

Build only what is needed to sell and support:

- 14-day trial limits;
- workspace and capture quotas;
- Studio/Agency entitlements;
- pilot application capture;
- onboarding checklist;
- activation metrics;
- conversion review;
- Stripe billing after plan boundaries are stable.

Commercial reason: revenue operations should reinforce proven usage, not precede it.

## 30-day execution

### Week 1

- Fix pooled database configuration and dependency security.
- Complete authenticated browser QA.
- Add a pilot readiness checklist.
- Recruit three agencies for observed workflow calls.

### Week 2

- Implement workspace/client foundations.
- Add reason-saved and client assignment to capture.
- Persist Router plans per workspace.

### Week 3

- Implement pattern/evidence objects.
- Produce the first cited brief from real captured items.
- Run it with one design partner on one live client.

### Week 4

- Add recommendation and outcome records.
- Package the £500 pilot around one repeatable workflow.
- Measure time to first board, time saved and client-output usefulness.

## Build/no-build rules

Build now:

- client isolation;
- provenance;
- evidence links;
- human annotations;
- cited outputs;
- outcome records;
- explicit capture;
- pilot conversion instrumentation.

Delay:

- generic chat;
- autonomous scraping;
- broad analytics dashboards;
- custom workflow canvas;
- white-label theming beyond export basics;
- SSO implementation before Agency Plus demand;
- native mobile app before mobile-share validation;
- universal AI scoring.

## Revenue path

### £10k MRR

- 20 Agency customers at £349 plus 20 Studio customers at £149 = £9,960 MRR.
- More realistic early mix: 15 Agency, 20 Studio and pilot/setup revenue.
- Required proof: one recurring workflow, two useful outputs monthly, internal owner.

### £25k MRR

- Expand successful agencies across clients.
- Raise average revenue through client/workspace usage and additional seats.
- Introduce Agency Plus only when permissions, API and support demand are observed.

### £50k MRR

- Standardize onboarding and evidence templates by agency niche.
- Add outcome integrations and cross-client learning controls.
- Develop partner/referral acquisition through strategists, consultants and agency networks.

## Success metrics

North-star:

- client decisions or deliverables backed by Recall evidence per active workspace per month.

Activation:

- first source captured;
- first “why saved” annotation;
- first client assignment;
- first pattern with three independent sources;
- first cited board/brief;
- first routed action;
- first recorded outcome.

Retention:

- active contributors;
- recurring client workflows;
- evidence reused;
- recommendations delivered;
- outcomes recorded;
- validated learnings reused.

## Principal risks

1. Becoming a bookmark manager with agency copy.
2. Building team collaboration without strict tenant/client isolation.
3. Generating briefs whose claims cannot be traced to evidence.
4. Treating AI labels as ground truth.
5. Expanding capture through unauthorized scraping.
6. Building generic agent orchestration instead of evidence-linked execution.
7. Delaying sales until the product appears complete.

## Final recommendation

The next paid version of Recall should be:

> A permissioned agency workspace where a team captures high-intent creative evidence, turns it into a cited recommendation, assigns the resulting work, and records whether it succeeded.

That product is narrower than the 100 alternatives, but it joins together the exact steps they leave fragmented.
